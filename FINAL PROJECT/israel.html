    • ISRAEL

    When you visit Israel, you typically visit the whole country in one visit so this country will all be on one guide separated by areas. I am splitting this into four sections: the two biggest cities - Tel Aviv and Jerusalem - and then the South and the North.

    What to Expect

    Language

    The dominant language in Israel is Hebrew. For those unfamilar with Hebrew, this will not come easy. Not only do Israelis speak impossibly fast in Hebrew, but the language is also totally unlike English, and even more, their letters are Hebrew letters, no where near the alphabet you are used to. However, you are generally in luck, as most people in the country speak English very, very well. This is mostly true in Tel Aviv, where I lived for six months and barely knew any Hebrew the entire time. Everyone speaks English in Tel Aviv. In Jerusalem and the other areas, it may be a little more difficult, but you will always find people who speak English. Another thing to note is that many, many Israelis speak Arabic. You will generally only see this in Arab towns and areas (like in the Arab area of the Old City in Jerusalem or Jaffa or in cities like Akko and the West Bank). But Arabs, too, speak Hebrew as well as a little bit of English, so it was never a problem.
